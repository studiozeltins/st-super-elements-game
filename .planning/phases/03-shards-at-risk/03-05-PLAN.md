  <action>Run the two-client playtest below and confirm all five scenarios; block until a human approves or reports a failure.</action>
  <what-built>
    Server-authoritative shard-at-risk economy: shard_drop table + collectShard reducer,
    applyDeathShardPenalty wired into PVE takeDamage, the worldTick player-death loop, and PVP
    attackPlayer (keeping the gem spill); client purple shard-drop renderer, lost/stolen/stole
    toasts, and counter loss/gain flash. Published additively to local.
  </what-built>
  <how-to-verify>
    Use two clients against the local server (webapp-testing / two browsers on the LAN host).
    1. PVP steal: Client A carries >=1 shard (transcend a C6 char or pick up a drop). Client B kills
       A. Expect: A's ◈ counter drops by 1 with the drain flash + "Zvaigžņu šķemba nozagta!"; B's ◈
       counter rises by exactly 1 with the pulse + "Nozagi zvaigžņu šķembu!"; NO ground drop appears.
    2. PVE drop: Client A (>=1 shard) dies to a PVE enemy/camp. Expect: a PURPLE shard_drop scatters
       at the death spot (distinct from gold gems — larger, brighter, sparkling); "Zvaigžņu šķemba
       nokrita" toast; no one is credited. After ~1.2s any player (including A) can walk over it to
       grab it (counter pulses, NO steal toast). A nearby camp must NOT vacuum it.
    3. Erosion (0 shards): Zero out A's shards, install a transcend level, then PVE-die. Expect: no
       counter change (0->0); the CharacterScreen "C6 · T{n}" badge decrements (transcend eroded).
       Die again with transcend 0 → exactly one C-level erodes (C6->C5) and no further.
    4. PVP empty victim: Client B kills a 0-shard Client A. Expect: B gains NOTHING (no phantom +1);
       total shards in the world do not rise.
    5. PVP shard carrier with 0 gems (shard-credit not gated by gem spill): Give Client A >=1 shard
       but ensure A carries 0 gems (spend/drop all gems first so the gem `stolen` amount is 0).
       Client B kills A. Expect: the shard STILL moves to B — B's ◈ counter rises by exactly 1
       (the "Nozagi zvaigžņu šķembu!" toast fires) and A's ◈ counter drops by 1; the shard is NOT
       destroyed. This proves the killer shard credit is not lost when the victim has no gems to
       spill (the credit is independent of the `if (stolen > 0)` gem guard). World shard total is
       conserved (A's -1 == B's +1).
  </how-to-verify>
  <resume-signal>Type "approved" if all five scenarios pass, or describe the failure.</resume-signal>
